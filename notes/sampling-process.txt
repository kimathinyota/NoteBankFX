<html dir="ltr"><head></head><body contenteditable="true"><p><font face="Arial">For time-variant sampled signal:</font></p><p><ul><li><font face="Arial">Sampled signal is achieved by multiplying the time-variant signal by the series of spikes of the transformed sampling function</font></li><li><font face="Arial">This gives samples that occur every delta t</font></li></ul><p><font face="Arial">For frequency-variant sampled signal</font></p><p><ul><li><font face="Arial">Convolve the spectrum of the time variant signal with the spectrum of the sampling function</font></li><li><font face="Arial">How: Take the spectrum of one and flip it along the horizontal axis and slide it across the other.</font></li><li><font face="Arial">By sliding the spectrum of the time-variant signal over the spectrum of the &nbsp;spikes (of the transformed sampling function) produces a spectrum where the spectrum of the original signal is repeated every (1/delta t) Hz</font></li></ul></p></p></body></html>
